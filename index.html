History

Just sit right back and you'll hear a tale...

  • 2009-2011

    The Beginning

    NYCHES was formally established in 1997 based on efforts of IT professionals from Syracuse University and Cornell University. These two individuals made contact with IT professionals from four other schools to pull together the first ever meeting. That small group decided that a forum was needed to provide an ongoing idea exchange.

  • March 2011

    A dialogue is Born

SIGUCCS attendees from these schools wanted to continue the 'SIGUCCS dialogs' throughout the year, so NYCHES has met a few times a year since 1997, with participation increasing to include dozens of schools across central and western NY state.

  • December 2012

    The Circle Widens

    In 1999, the founding members of NYCHES presented their story at a SIGUCCS conference. At that time, it was important to keep the group small and confined to the original eight member schools. But much has changed since 1999. People have changed jobs and left the group, but word about the group has spread. We now have participation from over twenty schools in the central and western NY region and over one hundred thirty active members subscribed to our listserv.

Hosting

Costs to attendees are minimal

Member schools volunteer to host the day-long meeting, and provide food for the attendees. There are no membership fees to be involved in NYCHES, so costs to attendees are minimized to travel expenses. Cost containment, particularly in the face of constricting travel budgets for many schools, made a regional conference an attractive option for schools that could not afford to send many people to national conferences. Even so, based on their experiences in NYCHES many members become active SIGUCCS attendees, and conversations started during SIGUCCS continue on in NYCHES meetings throughout the year.
